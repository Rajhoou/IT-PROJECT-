Western vs. Eastern Philosophy: Mind, Matter & Beyond

Western philosophy often builds upon logical analysis and individualism, seeking absolute truths through reason and empirical inquiry. Thinkers like Descartes emphasized the **dualism of mind and body**, while Kant introduced the limits of human knowledge through reason.

Eastern traditions, on the other hand, embrace holistic perspectives. Vedanta dissolves the self into **Brahman, the universal consciousness**, while Zen Buddhism finds wisdom in paradox and direct experience beyond rational concepts.

Where the West questions **“What can be known?”**, the East often asks **“What is beyond knowing?”** This contrast reveals deeper truths, showing that neither approach is complete without the other.
